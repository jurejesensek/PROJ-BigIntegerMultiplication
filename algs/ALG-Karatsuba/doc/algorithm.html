The Karatsuba algorithm if a divide-and-conquer algorithms for fast multiplication of numbers. It was discovered by Anatoly Karatsuba in 1960, and was the first multiplication algorithm that was asymptotically faster than the "grade school" method.

Time complexity: algorithm performs multiplication in \( O(n^{log_2 3}) \approx O(n^{1,585}) \).
